BUILDING
  • Each player builds a wall of 18 stacks of 2 tiles (36 total)
  • The right side of your wall goes on the inside of the other player wall --I
BREAKING
  • Dealer (E) rolls the dice
  • Count that many positions counter-clockwise starting w dealer (1, 5, 9)
  • Split that player'swall at dice number starting form the inside
  • Right side is the dead wall, left if the live wall
  • Dealer takes the first 4 tiles from the live wall
  • Each player takes 4 tiles until all players have 12
  • Everyone takes 1 more tile to get 13, dealer takes a 14th tile
BONUS TILES
  • Players reveal any Bonus tiles (Flowers / Seasons)
  • These are placed face up in front of them
  • Players draw replacement tiles from the dead wall, starting with E going counter-clockwise

GAMEPLAY

PONG
  • 3 of a kind
  • Can be completed with tiles from the wall or the latest discarded tile from ANY player
KONG
  • 4 of a kind
  • Can be completed with tiles from the wall or the latest discarded tile from ANY player
CHI
  • 3 sequential of the same suit
  • Can be completed with tiles from the wall or the latest discarded tile from the player from your left
EYES
  • 2 of a kind
  • Can be completed with tiles from the wall or the latest discarded tile from any player only if you're also declaring Mahjong
START
  • East goes first discarding a tile
  • Play goes counterclockwise
TURN

On a player's turn, they:

  • Draw a tile from the wall
  • Check if they can declare a win
  • If not winning, discard one tile face up in the center
  • Other players may claim the discarded tile under certain conditions
DRAW
  • Tiles are drawn from the open end of the wall
  • The wall is consumed clockwise, starting from the break
  • Each player takes one tile per turn unless claiming a discard
  • If player draws a flower/season they draw again from the dead wall
DISCARD
  • Discards are placed face up in the center
  • Once a tile is fully released from the hand, it cannot be taken back
GOAL

A complete winning hand consists of 14 tiles arranged as:

  • Four sets of three tiles each (either Pongs or Chows)
  • One pair (two identical tiles)
OTHER
  • Turn always ends with 13 tiles (until win)
  • Win > Pong / Kong > Chi
